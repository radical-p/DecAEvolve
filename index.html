Abstract

Finding mathematical relations underlying natural phenomena is a fundamental task in scientific discovery. Recent advances in evolutionary search with Large Language Models (LLMs) show great promise by leveraging their embedded scientific knowledge. However, discovering governing equations remains challenging due to vast combinatorial hypothesis spaces with exponentially many possible relations. Existing LLM-based approaches treat LLMs as static hypothesis generators unaware of the observed scientific system, leading to suboptimal and inefficient exploration that over-relies on internal priors.

DecAEvolve addresses this by combining granular feedback from symbolic term decomposition with LLM refinement through reinforcement learning fine-tuning. The framework unifies symbolic decomposition with test-time RL adaptation, enabling adaptive rather than static hypothesis generation. Across diverse scientific benchmarks, DecAEvolve improves both accuracy and discovery efficiency, reducing error by up to an order of magnitude compared to state-of-the-art baselines.

Why Equation Discovery Still Fails

LLM-based symbolic regression is promising because language models carry scientific priors and can synthesize executable equations. The paper argues that current methods leave two major sources of signal unused.

Static hypothesis generators

Existing LLM-SR systems keep the model fixed while searching. The model proposes programs from its prior, but it does not update its generation policy to match the specific observed system.

Coarse reward feedback

A scalar score tells the search which equation performed well, but not why. The model cannot see whether a sinusoidal term, power term, denominator, or interaction caused the improvement.

Core thesis: equation discovery improves when the LLM receives structural evidence about useful mathematical building blocks and adapts its weights to the target system during search.

Framework

DecAEvolve combines candidate generation, data-driven scoring, GRPO adaptation, symbolic term decomposition, and memory-guided evolution to make equation discovery adaptive and structure-aware.

Method Highlights

DecAEvolve extends LLM-SR with a closed loop: generate candidate equation programs, evaluate them against data, decompose successful programs into symbolic terms, fine-tune the generator with GRPO, and reuse annotated discoveries as future context.

Term-Level Contribution

Candidate programs are parsed into ASTs, split into atomic symbolic terms, ablated, re-optimized, and scored to reveal which mathematical components help.

GRPO Test-Time Adaptation

Generated equation candidates receive bounded data-driven rewards, then LoRA adapters update the policy while a KL term anchors the base model.

Decomposition-Guided Search

Annotated programs are stored in the population so later prompts include both successful hypotheses and fine-grained reasons they worked.

1 Generate

The LLM proposes executable Python equation skeletons with free parameters.

2 Optimize Parameters

Each skeleton’s continuous parameters are fitted with BFGS under an execution timeout.

3 Score

Fitness is computed from validation error and converted into a bounded reward.

4 Decompose

The AST is split into atoms; term and pairwise ablations estimate directional contribution.

5 Adapt + Evolve

GRPO updates LoRA adapters while annotated hypotheses enter the experience population.

Experimental Protocol

The experiments test whether adaptation and decomposition improve both final equation quality and search efficiency across scientific domains, model families, and in-domain versus out-of-domain evaluation.

Nonlinear Oscillators

Two second-order dynamical systems in displacement and velocity, designed to challenge models beyond memorized standard oscillator forms.

E. coli Growth

A biological growth task with density, substrate, temperature, and pH effects that require nonlinear composition rather than simple recall.

Stress-Strain

Experimental aluminum alloy tensile-response data across temperatures, giving a more realistic materials-science setting.

Baselines

Compared against GPlearn, PySR, SINDy, DSR, uDSR, NeSymReS, E2E, and LLM-SR under matched LLM call budgets.

Backbones and Metric

Evaluated on Qwen2.5 and Llama models from roughly 1B to 8B parameters using normalized MSE on ID and OOD splits.

The main pattern is not just that DecAEvolve reaches lower error, but that it does so on held-out distributions where static search often overfits or stalls.

What the Results Suggest

Decomposition is not just explanation

The term-level annotations become search signal: they make prior successes reusable at the level of mathematical components rather than whole equations.

Adaptation can substitute for scale

Targeted GRPO updates let smaller open models approach, and sometimes surpass, much larger general-purpose LLM-SR systems on these tasks.

Re-optimization matters

When terms are ablated, re-fitting the remaining parameters gives a cleaner estimate of structural contribution than freezing original parameters.

Main takeaway: DecAEvolve changes LLM-based equation discovery from static proposal generation into a feedback-driven scientific search process where the model learns from the data distribution, the symbolic structure, and the evolving population.
